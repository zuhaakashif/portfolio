Acne Classification (ML Research Project)

AcuCare Thesis

This project focused on developing and evaluating machine learning models for automated acne classification. Using image segmentation, feature extraction, and multiple classifiers, the system aimed to accurately distinguish between acne types (Nodule, Papule, and Pustule).

This project focused on the development and evaluation of machine learning models for automated acne detection and classification. The aim was to build a robust classification pipeline that could accurately distinguish between different acne lesion types such as nodules, papules, and pustules. Using a dataset of annotated acne images, multiple preprocessing steps were applied, including segmentation through clustering algorithms and feature extraction, to prepare the data for effective model training. The research was conducted as part of my undergraduate thesis to explore how ML-driven approaches can support dermatological analysis.

Two unsupervised segmentation pipelines were designed—K-Means and Fuzzy C-Means (FCM)—to isolate acne regions. From these segmented regions, color and texture features were extracted and refined through SelectKBest for feature selection. The selected features were then standardized using StandardScaler and evaluated across a range of classifiers including Extreme Learning Machine (ELM), Ensemble ELM, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Random Forest (RF), K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), and LightGBM. Extensive experimentation was performed with hyperparameter tuning and cross-validation to ensure fairness in comparison.

The experimental results demonstrated strong performance across both pipelines. The FCM pipeline achieved a test accuracy of 85.96%, while the K-Means pipeline achieved 84.21%, with ensemble classifiers providing stable results around 84–86%. Class-wise recall showed particularly high detection accuracy for pustules and papules, while nodules remained relatively harder to classify due to dataset imbalance. Overall, the research confirmed that ML models, particularly ELM and SVM, can provide reliable acne classification results, forming the scientific foundation for the development of the AcuCare mobile application.

Key Contributions

  • Designed a comparative study of six classifiers under two segmentation pipelines.
  • Achieved competitive accuracy despite limited dataset size.
  • Demonstrated the effectiveness of ensemble ELM and FCM segmentation in medical image analysis.
